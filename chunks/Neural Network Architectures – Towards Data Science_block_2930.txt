This implementation had both forward and backward implemented on a a NVIDIA GTX 280 graphic processor of an up to 9 layers neural network. In 2012, Alex Krizhevsky released AlexNet which was a deeper and much wider version of the LeNet and won by a large margin the difficult ImageNet competition. AlexNet scaled the insights of LeNet into a much larger neural network that could be used to learn much more complex objects and object hierarchies.
