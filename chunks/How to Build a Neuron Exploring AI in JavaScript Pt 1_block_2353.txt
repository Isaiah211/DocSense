If the cells don’t tend to fire together often, the connection weakens. In other words:
The key to synaptic plasticity is hidden in a pair of 20ms windows:
If the presynaptic neuron fires before the postsynaptic neuron within 20ms, the weight increases (LTP). If the presynaptic neuron fires after the postsynaptic neuron within 20ms, the weight decreases (LTD).
