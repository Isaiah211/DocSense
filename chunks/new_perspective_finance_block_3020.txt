This literature puts market mechanisms under the microscope and analyses specific
institutions of exchange and trading rules, especially their role in price formation; see O’Hara (1995). In earlier models of the price discovery process, dealers (or market makers) act as providers of
liquidity and set prices in order to control inventory levels. Recent work in market microstructure
focuses more on the impact of information on market prices, linking advances in the economics of
information, rational expectations and imperfect competition.
