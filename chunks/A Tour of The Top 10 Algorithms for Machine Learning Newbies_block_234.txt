After all the trees are built, predictions are made for new data, and the performance of each tree is weighted by how accurate it was on training data. Because so much attention is put on correcting mistakes by the algorithm it is important that you have clean data with outliers removed. A typical question asked by a beginner, when facing a wide variety of machine learning algorithms, is “which algorithm should I use?” The answer to the question varies depending on many factors, including: (1) The size, quality, and nature of data; (2) The available computational time; (3) The urgency of the task; and (4) What you want to do with the data.
