Let’s zoom out for a second to look at how other music services have tackled music recommendations, and how Spotify’s doing it better. Back in the 2000s, Songza kicked off the online music curation scene using manual curation to create playlists for users. This meant that a team of “music experts” or other human curators would put together playlists that they just thought sounded good, and then users would listen to those playlists.
