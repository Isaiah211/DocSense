Specifically, we’re classifying whether what’s streaming on a TV is a football game or an advertisement. Convolutional neural networks, which we used exclusively in our previous post, do an amazing job at taking in a fixed-size vector, like an image of an animal, and generating a fixed-size label, like the class of animal in the image. What CNNs cannot do (without computationally intensive 3D convolution layers) is accept a sequence of vectors.
